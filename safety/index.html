How Locals Ride

Safe Biking in Montréal

Montréal is a safe city to bike in, especially on protected routes. Here's how locals ride comfortably — and what to watch for.

The Protected Route Advantage

Montréal's safety advantage is infrastructure. Over 900 km of bike paths. Hundreds of protected lanes. When you stick to protected routes, you're essentially car-free.

Safest Protected Routes

Lachine Canal (completely car-free) • REV on Saint-Denis (bollard-separated) • De Maisonneuve corridor (one of longest in North America) • Verdun-LaSalle waterfront (zero traffic stress)

Intersections to Watch

⚠️ Saint-Laurent & de Maisonneuve

Busy crossing. Watch for turning cars. This is a high-traffic intersection.

⚠️ Parc Ave near Mount Royal

Merging traffic can be aggressive. Stay alert. The mountain draws confused drivers.

⚠️ Old Montréal Cobblestones

Historic but slippery when wet. Slow down. These surfaces are beautiful but challenging.

⚠️ Construction Zones

Montréal is always building. Infrastructure changes. Check routes before riding in construction areas.

Local Riding Etiquette

Signal Your Turns

Arm out to the side. This is standard practice in Montréal. Drivers expect it. Other cyclists appreciate it.

Pass on the Left, Announce Your Presence

Say "on your left" or ring your bell. Don't surprise slower cyclists. The Plateau especially has lots of casual riders.

Helmets Aren't Required (But Recommended)

Adults don't need helmets by law in Quebec. But they're a good idea. Most serious cyclists wear them. Kids need helmets.

Respect Pedestrians on Shared Paths

Lachine Canal has high foot traffic. Slow down near parks. Ring your bell. The path belongs to everyone.

Stop at Red Lights

Montréal cyclists mostly follow traffic rules. Red light = stop. This culture makes the city safe for everyone.

Bike Theft Prevention

Use a U-Lock

Cable locks are easily cut. U-locks are your real security. Price: worth it. Convenience: slight inconvenience. Prevention: essential.

Lock Frame AND Wheel

Thread the lock through the frame and at least one wheel. Lock to a fixed object (pole, fence, rack). Don't make it easy.

Don't Leave Bikes Outside Overnight

In busy areas (Plateau, Mile End, Downtown), don't leave bikes locked unattended overnight. The temptation is high.

BIXI Eliminates Theft

Pick up BIXI, ride, dock it. You walk away. Zero theft risk. This is one reason BIXI is so popular.

Register Your Bike

If stolen, registration helps recovery. Some shops and police departments maintain registries.

Winter Biking Safety

Montréal winters are harsh. But some cyclists ride year-round. If you do:

  • ✓ Studded tires are essential (grip on ice)
  • ✓ Lights are mandatory (short daylight)
  • ✓ Cleared paths like REV are rideable
  • ✓ Go slow. Visibility is low. Ice is unforgiving
  • ✓ Helmet use is strongly recommended

Ride Safe, Ride Smart

Montréal's bike infrastructure is excellent. Local culture respects cyclists. Follow these guidelines and you're safe.
