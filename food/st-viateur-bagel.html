Montreal's legendary bagel institution. Wood-fired oven. Warm bagels right from the fire. Generations of tradition. The perfect quick fuel stop or destination ride.

The Montreal Bagel

St-Viateur Bagel is not just a bagel shop. It's a Montreal institution. Operating since 1957, it's one of the oldest bagel shops in the city. They wood-fire bagels in a traditional oven. The bagels are warm, fresh, dense (proper Montreal style ‚Äî different from NYC). Perfect cyclist fuel.

This is a quick stop. In and out in 10 minutes if you want. Or sit on the curb, eat a warm bagel, watch Mile End go by. Either way, this is authentic Montreal and authentic cyclist culture.

Why These Bagels?

Wood-fired oven creates unique texture. Montreal-style bagels are denser, less puffy than NYC bagels. Boiled then wood-fired. Crispy outside, chewy inside. This is the real thing, made the traditional way for 65+ years.

The Bagel Stop Experience

The Space

Tiny. Historic. Narrow storefront. You can see into the kitchen. The massive wood-fired oven is visible. Smell of fresh bagels is incredible. Counter service only (no seating inside). Queue is usually out the door (but moves fast).

The Ritual

Order at the counter. Watch them grab a bagel from the oven (warm). Get it wrapped or ask for a bag. Pay. Leave or sit on the curb outside. Eat while walking or biking. Simple and perfect.

Mile End Context

St-Viateur Bagel is one of two major bagel shops on this street (Fairmount Bagel is nearby). Both are excellent. This one is slightly closer to the Plateau-Mile End route.

Pre-Ride vs. Post-Ride

Pre-ride: Perfect light fuel (30 min before ride). Post-ride: Great recovery snack. Quick protein and carbs. Both work.
